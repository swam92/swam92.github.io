COVID-19

March 18, 2020

Day four of my quarantine. Prior to that, I had been in a state of semi-quarantine and practicing a social distancing regime. My scope into daily life in Manhattan is limited by what I can see through my living room windows overlooking what is normally a busy intersection in the SoHo neighborhood. There are minimal people outside; the usual nightly music from the bars and restaurants, despite having been muffled recently by a zealous neighborhood association, is completely muted. However, there is the sound of an occasional car or truck driving by. I wonder how serious people around the city and the state have been in heeding the reccomendations of our governor and the more recent guidelines from the president. What is worrying me is that despite efforts by many of us to restrict the spread, their are still so many people who are out and about every day.

  • Medical professionals
  • MTA (including Metro North & LIRR), Port Authority, NJTransit workers
  • Critical government employees
  • Cashiers and other grocery store workers
  • Sanitation
  • Police
  • Fire
  • Chefs
  • Couriers
  • Air Traffic Control
  • Janitorial staff

These industries must include tens, if not hundreds of thousands of people in the NYC area. And not included are all of the people who are not heeding quarantine and social distancing guidelines, or are taking them seriously but are taking a much needed stroll or walking their dog. It seems like even with our best efforts the virus may still spread to many more people. On a lighter note- most of our snack food has been depleted. Everything remaining needs to be cooked or otherwise prepared somehow. Maybe this is a good thing though.

March 20, 2020

Day 6 of quarantine. Today I left the house to go for a walk, and it felt amazing. I truly had not left the house for a few days and this was much needed. But that sweet taste of the outdoors, of freedom, was so sweet, that I ended up going on two other walks. The direction that I walked in at each intserction was the one with the least people, as well as routes where there was no visible scaffolding. I avoided scaffolding because it tends to get dark and dingy underneath it, the air tends to feel stale, and it generally feels less clean that a sidwalk without scaffolding.

March 22, 2020

New York State is now on PAUSE. PAUSE is an acronym for "Policies Assure Uniform Safety for Everyone". In the coming days it will be interesting to see how seriously this is enforced. I hope that it will be used to break up social gatherings instead of targeting individuals getting some fresh air- but that remains to be seen. Its also unclear what punishment will be used to enforce this. Hopefully will work to slow to tranmission rate and give us some time to ramp up the hospital system and source adaquate supplies

There was also some news from Washington today that a coronavirus stimulus bill package worth 1.8 Trillion dollars failed to pass in the Senate in a 47-47 vote. I have been unable to source the primary text of this bill, but I can only assume it attempted to swindle in some inappropriate measures during a time of crisis. Apparently the bill extended a line of credit worth close to 500 billion dollars to the Federal Reserve to bailout unspecified industries. I consider this to be a serious waste of taxpayer money and another stain on the current administration. The fact that the government has not invoked its powers to help manufacture and distribute desperately needed medical supplies, while at the same time using trillions of taxpayer dollars to bail out industries, is deeply troubling. I think the juxtaposition of the proposed stimulus package, along with the guise of a "small federal government" being used to justify not invoking the Defense Production Act, shows the current administration to be hypocritical; hypocritical even when their "ideals" will be paid for up-front with American lives, and maintained by bleeding the country of future prosperity as our credit rating drops with our credibility, and interest payments pile up. The Trump Organization recieving any of this bailout money would be absolutely unacceptable. I hope the politicians representing us will do whats right.

S&P futures are currently down about 5%. Theres going to be a whole lot more economic pain tomorrow morning. I hope this doesn't force the Democrat's hand to pass an incomplete and harmful bailout plan.

On a more personal note, today marks a week almost completely quarantined in my apartment. There are definitely ups and downs, sometimes it can be deeply depressing, especially with everything thats going on, and sometimes it can be actually quite nice to have some actual free time, free from external pressures, to sit and think. But the main point of this post is that I am not excited at all about another week working from home. My apartment simply isn't large enough to really seperate my work life from my home life (which is my entire life now). I am hoping I will be able to slowly adapt to a working from home situation by sticking to a routine and just being dilligent with my state of mind as I approach each work day. Working as a software developer, I am granted a large amount of autonomy to begin with, and largely I am the person who makes sure I am accountable. And so working from home it can be easy to get sidetracked. I am intent on getting into a good routine with work both because it will help pass the time and well, because it is my job. The feeling of accomplishment after a day's work is better than not doing much work and being on youtube all day. Plus, I can watch youtube on my commute home.
